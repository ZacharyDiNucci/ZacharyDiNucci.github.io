About Me

Hello! My name is Zachary DiNucci. I completed my degree at Boise State University in their Games Interactive Media and Mobile Technology Degree also known as GIMM. GIMM is a program that introduces students to programming and 3d modeling through a lens of game design . My favorite topic that GIMM taught me was the game design do to the pr​ogramming required. This has been a highlight because I got to learn different useful languages for Unreal and Unity while learning how to design different mechanics.

During my time at Boise State I also completed a Minor in History. History is something that has always been an interest to me. I have enjoyed learning about the early development of civilizations such as the rise of early Islamic Civilization. A goal of mine is to be able to be apart of a game that uses history to tell a story.  

I enjoy traveling! I have had the unique opportunity to travel not only in the United States but also in Europe. I have visited 25 out of the 50 states and have a goal to visit all 50 States before I am 50. My favorite place in the United States to visit is Boston, Massachusetts purely due to the amount of history that resides there. 

Though the States have been my main source of travel I was also given the opportunity through a Junior high school program to visit France. Though my time in France was fast it had a big effect on me. The monuments, museums, buildings, and streets hold so much history. My favorite moments from the trip were visiting Omah Beach and the Palace of Versailles.   

Starting at a young age I have been involved in Music. My parents shared their interest in music, and it made me want to pursue different opportunities. A local nonprofit call Boise Rock School is where I first started my journey. I joined a band and was able to learn how to play the guitar. 

After learning and participating in different events my siblings and I decided to form our own band with one of our friends. This is where we made our own band called Know Reaction where I was able to help compose and learn music production and theory. We were able to record an EP called Clear to Me which taught me a lot of patience. While in the band we were invited to play in San Francisco to share our music with their local rock school. Though I am no longer in a band I still enjoy listening and playing music.

Robo Beatdown

Robo Beatdown is a game that I made using the Unreal Engine software. The game is inspired by games like SAS, Zombie Assault, and Hotline Miami. 

The assests used in the game was my own design that I modeled in Blender then using Unreal Engine I set up blueprint systems and level design.

Food Flingers

Food Flingers is a round based first person food fight. This game was created using Steam VR in Unity with assets created in Blender. 

The insperation for creating this game came from the release of the Valve Index. I wanted to make a game which used the finger tracking to interact with the world in a unique way. That is why I came up with a food fight.

Pig

Pig was a game made for a series of minigames where everyone traded off jobs. For Pig I was the artist and so had to create all the animations and art for the game using Blender.
The farmer was one of the first low poly animated people I have ever made. I even used a refined version of him in my VR game later.

Quack Attack

This was the 3rd game which my group worked on and it was the first time any of us had worked in Unreal. For this game I was to assist with the code and so created the code for the targets and the hud system. These were all using the blueprint system in Unreal Engine.

Multiplayer Bomber

Bomber was a multiplayer game which my group was unfortunately unable to finish. However it was a good lesson in multiplayer programming. For this game we used Unity with Pun 2 as the server stuff. One thing I learned from this game was to use code specificly made for multiplayer. This was because we were reusing code from a previous game which was all the stuff that didn't work.

Boise Schools AR

This last project was a Unity Augmented Reality app made in collaboration with a teacher from the Boise School district. This app was designed to help teach students with autism various skills such as how to order at a restaurant. I designed and coded all 3 minigames within this app with consulting from a special education teacher. Art and menu's were designed by other members of this group.
